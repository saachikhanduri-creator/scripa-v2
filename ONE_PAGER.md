# Scripa for Trades — One-Pager

## Positioning

**Scripa is voice documentation, built for one job: turning a technician's spoken job recap into a finished report before they've left the driveway.**

Not a general AI notetaker. Not a scheduling or invoicing platform. A single-purpose mobile app that a technician opens after every job, speaks into for 30–60 seconds, and gets back a structured, compliant job/inspection report — plus a plain-English summary ready to send the customer. Same core engine as the rest of the Scripa family (care, veterinary, pathology, therapy), repackaged 100% for the trades vocabulary, format, and workflow.

## ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

- **Buyer:** owner/operator or office manager at a small-to-mid HVAC, electrical, or plumbing service business (2–50 technicians).
- **User:** the field technician — currently handwriting notes on-site or doing paperwork at the end of the day, a task they actively avoid and often rush.
- **Trigger to buy:** inconsistent or missing job reports causing warranty disputes, poor customer communication, or technicians eating into billable hours on admin work.

## Why this profession first

1. Large, fragmented market (1M+ US trade technicians across HVAC/electrical/plumbing) with **no dominant vertical voice-documentation player** — voize, Heidi, Tandem, and Voquill are all clinical-only.
2. Documentation pain is directly tied to billable time — the ROI pitch writes itself: "give us 20 seconds of talking, get back an hour of your week."
3. Businesses already pay for field-service SaaS (ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro), so the buying behavior and budget line already exist — Scripa slots in as the tool that makes technicians and their reports faster and better, not a new category to justify from scratch.
4. Lower regulatory complexity than clinical documentation means a faster path to a genuinely useful MVP, while still being "regulated enough" (safety notes, code references, warranty documentation) to require a real structured format, not just a note.

## Product (MVP scope)

- Mobile-first web app: tap to record (or type/paste), speak the job recap, review the transcript, generate.
- One AI call produces **two outputs** from the same dictation: a structured job/inspection report (job info, equipment, work performed checklist, materials, issues, recommendations, safety concerns, follow-up flag) and a short client-facing plain-English summary.
- Built to demonstrate the "same engine, different product" thesis: the underlying speak → structure → generate pipeline is identical to what powers other Scripa verticals (care, vet, pathology) — only the vocabulary, schema, and branding change.

## Pricing (directional)

- **Per-technician seat, monthly SaaS**, e.g. **$29–49/technician/month**, tiered by usage (reports/month) and export integrations.
- Positioned below the cost of ~20 minutes of one technician's billable time per month — an easy "pays for itself in one job" argument for the owner.
- Free trial (first 2 weeks or first N reports) to let technicians experience the time savings before the business commits.

## GTM (Go-to-Market)

- **Direct outreach** to small-to-mid HVAC/electrical/plumbing service businesses via trade associations, local trade groups, and supply-house partnerships (distributors technicians already visit weekly).
- **Bottom-up technician adoption**: make the individual technician experience good enough that they push it up to their owner/office manager, similar to how voize and Heidi grew clinician-led.
- **Integration partnerships** (later stage) with field-service platforms (ServiceTitan, Jobber, Housecall Pro) — Scripa becomes the voice-to-structured-data layer that feeds the scheduling/invoicing tools businesses already run, rather than competing with them.
- **Content/demo-led selling**: short before/after demo videos (messy voice note → clean report) resonate directly with the core pain and are easy to produce and distribute to trade communities.
